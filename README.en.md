# LeafNote

[![日本語: Click to View](https://img.shields.io/badge/%E6%97%A5%E6%9C%AC%E8%AA%9E-Click%20to%20View-64748b)](./README.md)
![English: Current](https://img.shields.io/badge/English-Current-2ea44f)

LeafNote is a local-first notebook for growing your thoughts on your own machine instead of sending them to the cloud.

Quick notes, design drafts, research logs, tasks, code, tables, images, and Mermaid diagrams.
It gathers scattered working fragments into one quiet place and helps shape them into something you can revisit later.

Rather than flashy sync features or account integrations, LeafNote focuses on being fast to open, keeping your notes close at hand, and letting you export them as Markdown.

## Choose your UI language

LeafNote uses English as the default UI language, and the settings modal lets you choose English or 日本語.
Your choice is saved in IndexedDB, so the same language is restored the next time you open it.
The untouched welcome note also follows the selected UI language and switches between English and Japanese.

## Focus on writing

LeafNote asks for very little setup before you can start writing.
You do not need to create a heavy workspace or sign in every time.

Open it, and your notebook is ready.
From small notes to longer design documents, you can start writing in whatever order ideas arrive, then organize them as blocks later.

## Keeps Markdown flexible

Block editors are useful, but they can become hard to live with when your data feels locked inside them.
LeafNote treats Markdown as a natural format, making it easier to move your writing elsewhere and work alongside existing Markdown notes.

Your writing is not tied too tightly to a specific service or format.
That alone makes daily notes easier to keep.

## Strong for design notes

LeafNote is more than a plain text box.
You can structure your thoughts with headings, lists, todos, quotes, code, tables, images, and callouts.

It also supports Mermaid diagrams, so flows, states, relationships, schedules, and other ideas that are hard to express in prose can stay in the same note.
It works well for engineering specs, personal project planning, and organizing research.

## The comfort of local notes

Cloud notebooks are convenient, but not every note needs to live outside your machine.
Early ideas, rough work notes, personal knowledge, and things that are not ready to publish often feel better kept locally.

LeafNote is a tool for keeping those in-progress thoughts close.
You can write comfortably before deciding whether anything needs to be shared.

## Notes that grow

LeafNote is built not only for one-off notes, but also for notes that accumulate over time.
You can organize them hierarchically, find them through search, and export them as Markdown when needed.

Start rough, then shape things later.
That room to grow helps daily notes become your own knowledge base over time.

## Who it is for

- People who want something lighter than Notion but stronger than a plain memo app
- People who want to write in Markdown while still keeping notes visually organized
- People who want to keep personal development, design, study, and research notes locally
- People who hesitate to put early-stage thoughts in the cloud
- People who like portable, single-file workspaces
- People who want diagrams, code, tables, and tasks in the same context

## What LeafNote aims to be

LeafNote is not a huge information management system.
It is smaller and closer to your desk.

Ready when you want to write.
Quiet enough to stay out of your thinking.
Portable when you need to take notes elsewhere.

A calm and reliable Markdown notebook for everyday work.
That is LeafNote.
